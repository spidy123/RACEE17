Ir. Th. (Thijs) Edelkoort
1954

Function
Thijs works working as a consultant within the Organization and Housing Consultancy Business Unit.

Experience
In his work, Thijs focuses on both the micro and macro levels. He has extensive experience in assignments for which new concepts have been an important principle. He translates them into programs of requirements and feasibility studies that result in a business case, but also in concepts that are then further developed by architects. From these perspectives, Thijs generally work across discipline boundaries and views all facets of the assignment, while collaborating with requisite specialists.

Before coming to AT Osborne, Thijs worked at several consultancies as well as the State Property Agency. He has performed assignments in many sectors such as education, culture, municipalities, provinces, national government, semi-public institutions, housing construction, non-profit, industry and financial service provision.

Sustainability
Immediately after his graduation he was one of the competitors s of the European Solar Energy Design Contest. During his period at the State Property Agency he did research on Glass Houses and Atria in office buildings. In one of the projects at AT Osborne he was concept designer of the TNT Head Quarter, one of the first BREEAM excellent office buildings in the Netherlands.

Education
Thijs studied Architecture at TU Delft and graduated with Price. Then he developed his skills in Housing Management, which was still in its infancy at that time, and did postgraduate work in Corporate Real Estate and Value Management.

Projects
In his projects, Thijs likes working as part of a team, where he prefers to serve as the process manager. He supports the customer in the communication with all the stakeholders within his organization. This can include both managers and employees. In relevant projects, Thijs has found himself operating in the gray area between technique based on hard figures and activities involving employees, students or visitors involved in their primary processes.

Within AT Osborne, Thijs is responsible for creating a revised method for developing Programs of Requirements. The objective was to develop a document that all interested parties can read and understand, but is still robust enough to serve as a contract document for integrated contract forms. In the interim, he has amassed considerable experience in executing this method. Thijs is a certified Value Manager.

The method Thijs developed is called P360. This is a work method that not only records the requirements, but also specifies the ambitions behind these requirements: the why behind the imposed requirements. Thanks to this coupling, ambitions can be tracked even after some time and contradictory requirements can be traced back to the basic principles.

Managerial experience
Thijs is a member of the Supervisory Council of the Beauforthuis in Austerlitz, Zeist with a specific focus on building management. Prior to this, he was a member of the board of the Pallas Athene primary school in Amersfoort. In that function, he was responsible for initiating and realizing new construction in collaboration with a child care facility and the City of Amersfoort.

Relevant projects at AT Osborne

  • Chamber of Commerce (2016)

    • Developed a configuration concept for the ‘entrepreneur’s plaza’ that focused on the connection between the Service Model and the configuration, in the Blaak 40 national monument in Rotterdam.
    • Detailed the peripheral conditions for the ‘experience area’ in relation to the Kolb learning model

  • Ministry of Infrastructure and the Environment (2013)

    • Conducted a tender analysis for the Koningskade and Plesmanweg buildings in The Hague for centralizing housing
    • Detailed peripheral conditions for a configuration concept for anchor points for informal consultation and touch down workplaces.

  • Dudok Wonen, Hilversum (2010-2011)

    • Explored the possibilities of a housing concept in conformance with The New Work within the physical framework of The Milk Factory on the Larenseweg in Hilversum.
    • Detailed the usage options for an existing factory building that is on the list of national monuments.
    • Developed a program of requirements

  • The Ploeg, Bergeyk (2009-2010)

    • Quartermaster for the development and repurposing of the De Ploeg building for the Woninc Housing Corporation.
    • Developed a use concept for the building.
    • Conversations with potential parties who wanted to operate within this concept, and with the city government as an important stakeholder.

  • Central Judicial Collection Bureau (2008-2009)

    • Project leader for the Working Differently project
    • Developed an organization-oriented work environment concept.
    • Embedded the principles within the entire organization.
    • Managed the renovation project.
    • Implemented the adopted concept using workshops for employees, management teams and directors.

  • TNT-Real Estate (2007)

    • Developed a corporate standard for housing in Europe based on the Planet-me concept. CO2 reduction, specifically the question of how this must be incorporated into the design, was the focal point of this standard.
    • Developed a program of requirements based on TNT’s mission, strategy and corporate objectives.

  • North Community College (2006)

    • Developed a housing concept for integral housing for ROC of Amsterdam, a VMBO course for Bredero College and Building Academy.
    • Conducted a feasibility study including a financial analysis.
    • Added a number of (semi-) commercial functions in collaboration with the CZAN project developer.

  • Rabobank Westland; headquarters (2005-2006)

    • Developed an organization-oriented work environment-concept for the new Rabobank Westland headquarters to be constructed.
    • Made the preparations for implementation of the concept.
    • Tested the existing design, which was originally intended for housing the office in De Lier.

  • Foundation ANNO (2005)

    • Interviews with architects H. Henket, K. Kaan, S. Soeters, in which the urban developer situation and programmatic principles were used. Their reaction to these factors was the focal point of the interviews.
    • Developed and detailed the principles for the new construction concept for the Boulevard on the Anna van Buerenplein in The Hague.

  • Thomas Cook, headquarters Hoofddorp (2001)

    • Translated the directors’ strategic vision into a "vision paper" for the implementation of a new workplace concept. This document articulates the principles for the implementation, which focuses on retaining and integrating the personnel after the merger and relocation.

  • Euronext (previously AEX) (2000)

    • Managed the cultural shift after implementing the flexible workplace concept.
